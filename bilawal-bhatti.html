Pakistan all-rounder cricketer, Bilawal Bhatti who has played international as well as domestic cricket for Pakistan. The young cricketer was born on 17th September 1991, in Burewala, Punjab, Pakistan. He can bat as Right-handed and bowl as Right fast-medium cricketer in all format.

Career Journey

Bhatti played domestic cricket for the Sialkot Stallions squad for 2012 to 2015. Next, he then played as the all-rounder to represent Pakistan at the Asian Games held in Guangzhou, China. Bhatti won the bronze medal against Sri Lanka in the third-place playoffs of the game. He came into the ground directly to play international crickets and represented national team on 20th November 2013. He played T20 against South Africa. He played his One Day International debut for Pakistan against South Africa, held at Cape Town in 2013, where he gave a remarkable performance, and made his position immediately with both, bat as well as with the ball. He hit 39 runs off 25 balls, and also took 3 for 37 that let Pakistan to won the match.

As concerned as the test matches of play, he made his first test debut for Pakistan against Sri Lanka where he took 5 wickets in only 2 innings. In 2014, Bhatti played very well at the WT20 against Australia and made 40 runs in one over on the bowling of Maxwell and Finch.

Throughout his domestic career, one of the best performances was at the 2018–19 Quaid-e-Azam Trophy, where Bhatti was one of the leading wicket-taker with 36 wickets in only ten matches for Sui Northern Gas Pipelines Limited.

As we concerned about the PSL, Bhatti was picked up by the Lahore Qalanders cricket franchise in 2017 for the 3rd season of the Pakistan Super League (PSL). Unfortunately, his shoulder injury made him unable to play in the league. Currently, the player is making his debut for the Quetta Gladiator franchise in the player auction of the 2020 Pakistan Super League.
